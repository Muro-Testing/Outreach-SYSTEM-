Plain text emails consistently outperform HTML-formatted emails in cold outreach (B2B sales, networking, etc.), according to multiple 2025–2026 studies and internal benchmarks.
This is the clearest, most data-backed finding across sources like Hunter.io, Warmforge, HubSpot historical tests, Lemlist, and others. Plain text wins on deliverability, open rates, reply rates, and overall perception as a personal, human message rather than a bulk blast.
Key Performance Data: HTML vs. Plain Text

Deliverability and bounces: Hunter.io’s 2025 analysis of 2.2 million cold emails showed HTML campaigns bounce 674% more (or +652% higher bounce rate) than plain text. Plain text reduces bounces by ~86.7%. HTML triggers spam filters more due to formatting code, tracking pixels, links, and styling.
Open rates and clicks: Plain text emails show 21–42% higher click-through rates (sometimes up to 51% in image-heavy comparisons). HTML with rich elements (images/GIFs) drops open rates by 23–37%.
Reply/engagement rates: Plain text drives 20–40% higher replies in cold outreach tests. It feels authentic and conversational. HTML often gets flagged as promotional.
Exceptions: One smaller study found branded HTML slightly better (higher inbox/open/reply rates), but this is an outlier—most cold outreach data strongly favors plain text. HTML performs better for warm audiences, newsletters, or when heavy branding/visuals are needed.

Why plain text wins for outreach: It mimics a natural one-to-one email (like texting), avoids spam triggers, loads instantly on all devices, and builds trust. Recipients spend only seconds scanning emails—simplicity cuts through.
Images, Profile Pictures, and Logos: Minimal Use (1 Relevant Image) Often Boosts Replies
You can include images without tanking performance—if done right. Pure plain text has no images, but many high-performers use minimal HTML (or multipart emails) with exactly one optimized image.

One image effect: Lemlist’s analysis of millions of cold emails found +20% reply rates with one image vs. none. Open rates were identical; no meaningful deliverability hit.
B2B A/B test (10k+ emails): Image-enhanced emails had nearly identical opens (61.7% vs. 61.4% plain text) but higher replies (8.7% vs. 6.1%). Relevant images (product screenshots, value visuals) helped; “markety” flashy banners/infographics hurt (opens dropped to 47%, replies to 3.2%).
Profile pictures/headshots: These humanize the sender (“puts a face to the name”) and are frequently recommended in signatures for trust and response rates. They work well as the single image—professional, small (e.g., 100–400px), and relevant.
Logos: Small company logos in signatures can lift opens (6–39% in some consumer/brand studies) by adding professionalism/branding. Keep it to one image total and non-intrusive.
What hurts:
Multiple images (>2) or GIFs → lower replies/opens and higher risk.
Large/attached files → bounces rise (use hosted/linked images that render inline).
Flashy or promotional visuals → spam/promotions tab.


Sweet spot construction: Plain-text body (no formatting) + minimal HTML signature with one small relevant image (headshot or logo, hosted externally). Many tools send this as multipart (plain + light HTML fallback). Avoid tracking pixels, heavy styling, or buttons if deliverability is your top priority.
How Top-Performing Outreach Emails Are Constructed (Exact Patterns)
From 2025–2026 benchmarks and tests (Instantly, Snov.io, Belkins, Growth List, etc.):

Format: Plain text (or extremely minimal HTML) is the default for elite campaigns (top performers hit 5.5–10%+ reply rates vs. ~3.4% average).
Length/structure: 50–125 words (1–2 short paragraphs, sometimes <100 characters). Short, scannable, conversational. One clear CTA.
Signature: Simple text (name, title, company, contact) ± one small image (headshot/logo). No quotes, excessive links, or heavy branding.
No extras: No attachments, no embedded GIFs/videos, minimal/no tracking in the body (add only if tested).
Personalization lift: Far more important than format—advanced personalization (beyond first name) can 2–3x replies or boost to 18%.

This mirrors what data shows drives replies: personal, short, relevant, human.
Bottom Line & Actionable Advice

Best overall: Plain text body + optional one small relevant image (headshot or logo) in signature. This combination maximizes deliverability while boosting engagement.
Test it: A/B test plain text vs. your current HTML setup on your own list—results vary slightly by audience, industry, and sender reputation.
Format is only part of it: Even the best format fails without list hygiene, warm-up, strong personalization, short subject lines (<7 words), and good timing (Tue–Thu, ~1 PM recipient time).

These insights come from large-scale 2025–2026 data (millions of emails analyzed).
